Why this blog

You certainly ended up here while casually –stalking– checking my Linkedin Page and might be wondering what you will find here.

I always wanted to start a blog for several reasons:

  • I thought it would be fun
  • Like you, a lot is going on up there and I want to keep my thoughts clear and organized on some subjects.
  • I realized I had gathered a significant amount of web resources and I wanted to start organizing them: if they are not worth sharing, they are not worth keeping
  • I “bombed” my first carrier in my early years (I am not that old) and I want to share all the lessons learned I wish someone had told me when I first graduated
  • I want to be able to share a page on my subjects of interest with like-minded people and maybe start interesting (non/for-profit) projects together!
  • I did spend quite some time abroad: Some reviews of each country/city I lived in and some tips on how to move abroad
  • by publicly committing to stuff, I will be peer-pressured to actually achieve what I intended to do
  • I just want to get better at writing and practice is key

Most of the blog posts will be based on my experience and my own opinion. I will try to stay neutral but there are unavoidable biases to keep in mind when reading them. Some posts will definitely look unfinished/unpolished: I see them as being permanently drafts. Rather than aiming for perfection in a first trial (which is unrealistic), delivery and iterations are what I will aim for. I will keep improving each article, rewrite some parts if necessary, correct typos etc.

Overall they will become a memoir I can cringe on later as I get … “wiser”? But if those posts can help even a single person, I will see this blog as being a successful project.

I will mainly focus on the English side to reach a wider audience and might start writing posts in other languages later!

Cheers, Hiro
