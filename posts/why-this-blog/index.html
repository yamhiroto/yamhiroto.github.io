Why this blog

You certainly ended up here while casually –stalking– browsing my LinkedIn page, and you might be wondering what’s in store.

I’ve always had the idea of starting a blog for a few reasons:

  • Firstly, I thought it would be enjoyable.
  • Similar to you, there’s a lot happening in my mind, and I want to keep my thoughts organized on certain subjects.
  • Recognizing that I’ve amassed a significant collection of web resources, I felt it was time to start organizing them. If they aren’t worth sharing, they aren’t worth keeping.
  • I “bombed” my first career in my early years (I am not that old),and I want to share the lessons I learnedâ€”stuff I wish someone had told me when I first graduated.
  • I want to create a space to share my interests with like-minded people and perhaps embark on interesting projects, be they non-profit or for-profit.
  • Having spent some time abroad, expect reviews of each country/city I’ve lived in and some tips on navigating life abroad.
  • By making public commitments, I’m hoping the peer pressure will drive me to actually achieve what I set out to do.
  • I’m also keen on improving my writing skills, recognizing that practice is essential.

Most of the blog posts will stem from my experiences and personal opinions. I’ll strive for neutrality, but it’s crucial to keep in mind that some biases are unavoidable. Certain posts may appear a bit rough around the edgesâ€”intentionally so as I see them as being permanent drafts. Rather than striving for perfection in the first go, I’ll focus on delivery and making improvements over time. I’ll keep refining each article, rewriting parts if needed, and fixing any typos.

In the grand scheme of things, these posts will likely become a memoir I can look back on and maybe cringe a bit as I become… “wiser”? But if these posts can help even one person, I’ll consider this blog a success.

My primary focus will be on the English side to connect with a broader audience, but who knows, I might dabble in writing posts in other languages down the line!

Cheers, Hiro
